I ran across the miniseries "Devs" the other day.  I like that genre, anything Sci-Fi Thriller right up my alley.  And of course I love Nick Offerman. At first I thought Alison Pill was Kristen Bell.  I enjoyed the Good Place for a most of  it's run.  I didn't see her name though, in the credits.. and I looked up who Alison Pill was...  I had seen Alison Pill in Star Trek - Picard!  The doctor, the cybernetisist!  Of course.. her time as a Borg came back and it was perfect.  I had already fallen in love with her.  I didn't know the other actors.

Devs had the feel of "Ex Machina" - I had never looked up the writer at the moment, but now realize it is the same writer as Ex Machina.  Michelle Day's name caught my attention first. I looked her up and saw she had done the set decorations just like in this movie.  I loved it!

During the time in and around the offices, and the segue between scenes.. was redwoods and the buildings. I told my lady, who was watching it with me... I swear that looks like UCSC!!  I love the campus... so I had to google the filming location and omg.. it was UCSC.  I used to be an IT guy on the campus and McHenry was where I had spent a lot of time...walking back and forth from Science Hill and of course that field between to the music rooms and the chancellors house was one of my favorite places to go.  I immedietly had to pull up google maps and show my lady the places.  There is nothing like the brutalist architecture contrast against the redwood forest and immense upper field that gives way to the ocean.  And the Quarry!  I miss it all so very much.  I dream of being back there.

I loved to make field recordings with my stereo microphone and DAT recorder at the time.  Professor David Cope worked there. He was a groundbreaking pioneer in AI-generated and algorithmic music.  As well as Professor Peter Elsea, director of the electronic music program and a Max/MSP guy. I love Max and PureData, both created by Miller Puckette, but we'll get into that later.  David Cope and Peter Elsea were pioneers of their time and they both worked when I was a there.  I made a field recording inside the hallways outside David Cope office and the practice rooms and outside their buildings and such. I adored it.  I wish I could have been a student there.  Jaron Lanier had even given a talk at UCSC and one of the questions from the crowd was what he thought of David Cope's AI/algorithmic/generative music.  Jaron had said he didn't think it was music.  I disagreed with him, but I didn't say anything, I still look up to him.

Also during this time I had been reading, or trying to read, books by David Bohm and his theories on quantum mechanics and how we might interpret them. I became fascinated with Bohm's ideas of the implicate and explicate order. And how nouns don't really work well for describing the world around us and instead we should be using verbs for everything, but that kind of breaks English in a way.  Like how everything is in motion and is different from when it is at this point and then at another point in time.  In the miniseries this concept might be represented when Lilly's dad mentioned how when we enter a stream it's never the same stream and we are never the same person entering that stream.

Already enamored with the details of the miniseries, I was floored when they played Steve Reich's tape loops! Loving that type of music like John Cage, David Tudor, etc, I also loved Steve Reich! In fact I used this specific tape loop as homework for a synthesis/music class at Cabrillo college.  I have "to show them, to show them, to show them" laser printed into my brain tissue.

Another weird and quirky fact is that I lived in the house where the physicist developed a computer that fit inside his shoe so he could win at gambling at [707 Riverside Ave](https://historyrighthere.blogspot.com/2017/01/the-first-geek-house-in-santa-cruz.html).  


I made the mistake of reading what the internet thought of the miniseries titled Devs.

I read that Alison Pill's acting was horrible and her face was like a brick.  Before I even read that... I thought her acting was amazing.  I thought maybe she was an android for a second, but then figured that this didn't venture into that realm.  Instead I realized, after a few episodes.... that her character had watched the same iteration of the future over and over times that this was her going through the motions... she was bored of this shit already.  She had already gone through the shock and egoloss that occured when she found out that she has no choices and that everything is determined. It was perfect... her lack of any feelings, just following along with the truama of knowing what happens next.  Thats the way Nick Offerman seemed to act also, but his character dealt with the boring future with a bit more whimsy.  

Come to find out the project's pets name was really Deus and knowing prior movie from the same author was Ex Machina ... Dues Ex Machina.. is kind of neat.
